To have official seed samples tested for physical purity, germination and/or viability,Department of Agriculture, Land Reform and Rural Developmentsamplers must draw the samples.

The official seed samples must comply with Plant Improvement Act (PIA) standards. If seed does not comply, they must be withdrawn for sale or re-labelled.

Note: Reports on tests conducted are sent to the sampler who drew the sample. If you want these results, you must pay the applicable tariff and a seed analysis report will be issued.

Remember to include the invoice number for reference purposes. Pay the seed testing tariff and send proof of payment to OSTL for the release of the report.

The various crop types have different test conditions and the duration of a test can vary from a few days to a few weeks.
